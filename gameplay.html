Suit Runs

Gameplay

Each player on their turn will play one card from their hand and place into one of their own run piles or the shared discard piles (respective of that suit) and then pick up a card either from the shared discard piles (if any cards are present) or the draw deck (picking up a card marks the end of your turn). When the last card has been drawn from the draw pile the game immediately ends (this means the last card is not playable).

Each card has a respective point value except for face cards and aces (these are multiplier and wild cards), 2 is worth 2 points and 10 is worth 10 points. Face cards multiply the final point total at the end of the game.

Ace is wild, King is a multiplier, Queen is a multiplier, Jack is a multiplier, 10 is a point card

Except for the discard piles, cards must be laid down so that each new card in the run is higher than the previous for that suit (can play a 5 on a 2 but not a 2 on a 5).

Aces act as wild cards and when played will immediately act as the “next” card up, up to the value of 10. (Think of an ace as a way to bypass a lower card you don’t yet have, if the ten has already been played then the ace is worthless as there is no nigher card than a ten. aces can only act as point cards, not multiplier cards). Here the ace is acting as a 4.

Multiplier cards are played before the point cards (think of them having point values of zero) and more than one multiplier can be played in a run. They also don't need to be played in the order JQK.

Once a card has been played in your own run pile it cannot be removed. When starting a pile an automatic 20 point deduction is applied to that pile thus the equation to calculate your total for that pile at the end of the run is:

(card_points - 20) * (1 + multiplier_cards) = new_total

This means that if your card points don't atleast match 20, then you will score negative in that run. Note: you can start as many or as few runs as you want.

Sample Senario 1:

If you have the KQ234A79 in a run then you first add 2 + 3 + 4 + 5(ace is in this case worth 5) + 7 + 9 = 30, you also have two multiplier cards (K and Q), thus your point total is:

(30 - 20) * (1 + 2) = (10) * (3) = 30

You have scored 30 points in that pile.

Sample Senario 2:

If you have the J3A7 in a run then you first add 2 + 3 + 4(ace is in this case worth 4) + 7 = 14, you also have one multiplier card (J), thus your point total is:

(14 - 20) * (1 + 1) = (-6) * (2) = -12

You have scored -12 points in that pile.

Totaling up points

Each pile is tallied up and the scores are combined, the game consists of 3 rounds, (or any number you please). the winner is whom ever achieves the highest score at the end of the game.
