Strategies

Scoring High Is The Same As Your Opponent Scoring Low

Your objective is to score more points so it is in your best interest to avoid handing over valuable cards to the discard pile knowing your opponent will pick them up. Try to only discard cards your opponent can’t use such as a multiplier card when the player has already placed a point card in that suit.

In this example you know that player 2 is invested in diamonds so it would be wise to not discard your 6 of diamonds but rather the Q of diamonds as your opponent can't use it.

If you think you can’t collect any more points, then you should only draw cards from the draw pile, this will make the round end faster and minimize the points your opponent will be able to play (remember your opponent can’t play the last 6 cards in their hand when the game ends).

How To Start

To decide which pile to start can be tricky, having a lot of high cards is a good indication you will come out ahead in the run but also means starting the pile without the smaller point cards or the multiplier cards which hurt your potential. Having a lot of smaller cards or multiplier cards is good for starting a pile but is risky as there is no guarantee you will collect the higher cards. This can be used to your advantage as a sneaky way to harm your opponent. If you have some of the high cards and some multiplier cards in a particular suit then try baiting your opponent with multiplier cards to start that pile and then hold off the higher cards, this will increase the chances of their run going negative.

Plan Your Turns

Take caution when planning out your runs, especially later in the game. one of the many mistakes players make is overestimating how many turns they have left, remember your opponent can attempt to end the game early by only drawing from the draw pile. so plan out your run with limited turns in mind. this can mean sacrificing low cards you already have in order to have enough turns to play the bigger cards. example: say you have laid down already the QJ2345 and have in your hand the 67910. if you know you only have 3 turns left, then it would be wise to skip the 6 and go straight to the 7 to ensure you can play the 10.
